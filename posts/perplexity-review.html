What Is Perplexity?

Perplexity is an AI-powered search engine that combines real-time web search with LLM reasoning. Instead of giving you ten blue links, it reads the search results, synthesises them, and gives you a direct answer with inline citations you can click to verify.

It uses its own search index combined with models from OpenAI and Anthropic. The Pro tier unlocks deeper research capabilities, file uploads for document analysis, and higher daily query limits.

What I Tested

  • Daily search: Used Perplexity exclusively for two weeks instead of Google
  • Deep research: Multi-step research into AI training costs, GPU availability, market analysis
  • Academic work: Literature review, fact-checking citations, summarising research papers
  • Collections: Organising research into folders for long-running projects

Performance Breakdown

1. Daily Search ★★★★☆

For 80% of daily searches — "what is X", "how does Y work", "latest news on Z" — Perplexity is better than Google. You get a clear, sourced answer immediately without wading through SEO-optimised blog posts. The citations are a game-changer for trust.

Where it falls short: local searches ("coffee shops near me"), shopping ("best laptop under £1000"), and very recent breaking news. Google still wins for those.

2. Deep Research ★★★★★

Pro's "Deep Research" mode is genuinely impressive. I asked it to research the GPU shortage's impact on AI startups and it produced a multi-page report with market data, quotes from industry reports, and a timeline of events — all properly sourced.

The key advantage is iterative depth. You can ask follow-ups and Perplexity remembers the research thread, refining and expanding on previous answers. For a student writing an economics essay, this alone is worth the subscription.

3. Collections ★★★★☆

Collections let you save and organise searches into folders. Great for long-running research projects, though the organisation could be more flexible (no tagging, no nesting).

4. File Uploads ★★★★☆

You can upload PDFs, images, and documents for analysis. Perplexity reads them and can answer questions about their content. It's not as deep as Claude's document analysis, but for quick extraction and summarisation it's very useful.

✅ Pros

  • Direct answers with citations you can verify
  • Deep Research mode produces genuinely useful reports
  • Excellent for learning and understanding complex topics
  • Collections for organising research
  • Free tier is genuinely useful

❌ Cons

  • $20/mo Pro is pricey for students
  • Struggles with local and shopping queries
  • Still hallucinates occasionally (cites pages that don't exist)
  • Pro search limit of 500/day can run out
  • No offline mode

Pricing

  • Free: Unlimited basic searches, limited Pro searches per day
  • Pro ($20/mo): Unlimited Pro searches, file uploads, deep research mode, higher priority
  • Student discount: Available — check their pricing page

Alternatives

  • Google + ChatGPT — Free and familiar. ChatGPT with web search covers similar ground
  • You.com — Another AI search engine with a generous free tier
  • Consensus — Academic-focused AI search, excellent for research papers

🏆 Final Verdict: 8/10

Perplexity is my daily driver for research and learning. The free tier is good enough to know if it's for you. Pro is worth it if you do regular deep-dive research — students writing essays, professionals tracking industries, anyone who wants to understand a topic quickly and accurately.
